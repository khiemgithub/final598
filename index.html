About Cultivate

Located on the street level of Elm Hall, Cultivate is a special destination for students, faculty, staff and guests who want to experience full-service dining with a nod to locally sourced fresh food and a community feel. The menu celebrates new discoveries and memories of food, and features a Pacific Northwest twist on regional comforts. Try our savory signature house-smoked pastrami or shrimp po'boy sandwich. Vegetarians can revel in the fresh, crisp tastes of our seasonal market salad, veggie quesadillas or the mac bar.


John Vandeley

John is our dedicated Head Chef. He has more than 15 years of culinary experience, having led numerous restaurants across the globe. In his most recent gig, John managed the kitchen for Hyatt Park Bangkok, a five-star luxury restaurant well known for its fine Eurasian dishes. Now living in Seattle, John sees this as an exciting opportunity to truly work with locally sourced fresh food - his lifelong passion. With his knowledge of international cuisinary, he's excited to bring to the Cultivate tables dishes you will love to eat and tell others about.


Adam Lee

As the Lead Manager, Adam brings to Cultivate 10 years of culinary management experience. Besides being a food lover, Adam's management philosophy starts with the client. The client is always right, and the existence of Cultivate relies on the full satisfaction of the clients. As a manager, Adam also tries to build a strong bond with his staff. Now in his fifth year at Cultivate, the restaurant has become a second home to him. He welcomes you to come and try out our diverse menu, with new and exciting dishes being introduced to the tables everyday.

Our Specialties

Our menu changes with the seasons, and you'll find exciting new dishes to try that are featured on the daily fresh sheet everyday. Our ingredients are all locally influenced, seasonally motivated, and sustainably minded. Check out some of the specialty dishes that have helped made Cultivate a popular choice for students, faculty, and staff at the University of Washington - and elsewhere!
